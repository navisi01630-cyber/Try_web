How to Pray (Salah)

Step-by-step guide to performing the Islamic prayer

How to Pray (Salah)

Salah is the second pillar of Islam, performed five times daily. This one-page guide explains the steps clearly.

1. Preparation

  • Perform Wudu (ablution) in the prescribed order.
  • Wear clean clothes and choose a clean place to pray.
  • Face the Qiblah (Kaaba in Makkah).
  • Pray at the correct time for each Salah.
  • Form the intention (Niyyah) in your heart.

2. Step-by-Step Salah (2 Rak'ah Example)

  1. Takbir al-Ihram: Raise hands, say "Allahu Akbar," place right hand over left on chest.
  2. Opening Supplication (optional): "Subhanaka Allahumma..."
  3. Recitation: Surah Al-Fatihah + another surah.
  4. Ruku (Bowing): Hands on knees, back straight, say "Subhana Rabbiyal 'Azim" ×3.
  5. Standing after Ruku: Say "Sami'a Allahu liman hamidah," then "Rabbana lakal-hamd."
  6. Sujud (Prostration): Forehead, nose, palms, knees, toes on ground; say "Subhana Rabbiyal A'la" ×3.
  7. Sitting between Sujud: Say "Rabbighfir li."
  8. Second Sujud same as first.
  9. Second Rak'ah: Repeat steps 3-8.
  10. Tashahhud: Recite At-Tahiyyat; if final rak'ah, add Salat Ibrahimiyya.
  11. Tasleem: Turn right then left, saying "As-salamu 'alaykum wa rahmatullah."

4. Tips for Better Salah

  • Focus your heart and mind on Allah.
  • Pray without rushing.
  • Understand the meanings of recitations.
  • Maintain correct posture and pronunciation.

Hadith Collection

The Hadith are the recorded sayings, actions, and approvals of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). They serve as a key source of guidance for Muslims, alongside the Quran. Each Hadith is carefully transmitted through a chain of narrators (Isnad) and is classified based on authenticity.

Categories of Authenticity

  • Sahih – Authentic and reliable.
  • Hasan – Good, but slightly lower in authenticity than Sahih.
  • Da'if – Weak, due to gaps or weakness in the chain of narration.

Famous Hadith Collections

  • Sahih al-Bukhari – Widely regarded as the most authentic collection.
  • Sahih Muslim – Another highly authentic source.
  • Sunan Abu Dawood – Focused on Islamic law and daily practice.
  • Jami` at-Tirmidhi – Includes both Sahih and Hasan narrations with commentary.
  • Sunan an-Nasa'i – Known for strict authenticity standards.
  • Sunan Ibn Majah – Covers many topics, including moral teachings.
